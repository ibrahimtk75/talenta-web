Football Positions Explained — Which Position Suits You?

By Talenta · Updated June 2026 · 5 min read

Choosing the right position can transform your game. Play where your natural strengths shine, and everything gets easier — you make better decisions, enjoy it more, and scouts notice you faster. Here's a simple breakdown of every football position and the skills each one needs.

Goalkeeper

The last line of defence. Goalkeepers need sharp reflexes, brave decision-making, good handling, command of the box, and increasingly, the ability to play out from the back with their feet. Height and concentration help. If you love shot-stopping and stay calm under pressure, this is your role.

Defenders

Defenders stop the opposition from scoring. There are two main types:

  • Centre-Back (CB) — strong, good in the air, reads the game, wins tackles, and stays composed. The defensive anchor.
  • Full-Back (Right-Back / Left-Back) — quick and fit, defends the flanks but also overlaps to support attacks. Modern full-backs need real stamina.

Midfielders

The engine of the team — midfielders link defence and attack:

  • Defensive Midfielder (DM) — shields the defence, wins the ball back, and keeps possession ticking.
  • Central Midfielder (CM) — the all-rounder: passing, work-rate, tackling and tempo. Great vision is key.
  • Attacking Midfielder (AM) — creates chances, threads through-balls, and chips in with goals. Creativity and flair live here.

Forwards

The players who win games at the sharp end:

  • Winger (Right / Left) — fast and tricky, beats defenders one-on-one, and delivers crosses or cuts inside to shoot.
  • Striker / Centre-Forward — the finisher. Clinical in front of goal, smart movement, and the composure to score under pressure.

How to find your best position

Be honest about your strengths. Quick and direct? Try winger or full-back. Strong and commanding? Centre-back. Great passer who reads the game? Midfield. Cool finisher? Striker. Many players also have a clear skill level — beginner to professional — and the right level of football to develop in. Don't be afraid to try a couple of roles before settling.
