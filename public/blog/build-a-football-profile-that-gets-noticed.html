How to Build a Football Profile That Gets You Noticed

By Talenta · Updated June 2026 · 4 min read

Before a scout ever watches you play live, they judge you on your profile. In a few seconds they decide whether to keep watching or scroll on. Treat your football profile as your first trial — here's how to make it count.

1. Lead with a strong highlight video

Video is everything. Keep your highlight reel to 60–90 seconds, start with your best moment, and show your face clearly at the beginning so scouts can connect the player to the clips. Good light, a steady phone and clear action beat fancy editing every time.

2. Get your basics exactly right

  • Position — be specific (e.g. "Left Winger", not just "Forward").
  • Age & date of birth — honesty here builds trust; age fraud destroys it.
  • Foot, height and current club/academy.

3. Show real, believable stats

List appearances, goals, assists and pass accuracy. Don't exaggerate — a verified, modest record beats unbelievable numbers. Where possible, ask a coach to confirm them.

4. Add a short, honest bio

Two or three lines: where you play, your biggest strength, and your goal. Keep it humble and specific. "Quick left winger, strong on the counter, looking to join an academy" works far better than vague claims.

5. Make it easy to find and contact you

A profile that only lives on your phone helps no one. Put it on a platform built for discovery, like Talenta, where clubs and academies can search by position, age and country and message you directly — for free.
