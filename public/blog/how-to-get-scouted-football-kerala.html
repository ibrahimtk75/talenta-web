How to Get Scouted as a Footballer in Kerala (2026 Guide)

By Talenta · Updated June 2026 · 5 min read

Kerala produces an extraordinary amount of football talent — but most of it never gets seen. Across villages, schools and small clubs, thousands of skilful young players never reach a professional academy or club, simply because nobody with the power to sign them ever watches them play.

The good news: in 2026, getting discovered no longer depends only on who you know. Here's a practical, step-by-step guide for young footballers in Kerala who want to get scouted.

1. Understand what scouts actually look for

Scouts don't just want goals — they want repeatable ability. They look for first touch, decision-making, work-rate, both feet, and how you perform against good opposition. Before chasing trials, be honest about your strengths and build clips that prove them.

2. Film yourself — the right way

Your phone is enough. Capture two kinds of video:

  • A highlight reel — 60–90 seconds of your best moments, with a clear shot of your face at the start.
  • One-take drills — unedited clips (wall passes, dribbling through cones, a 30m sprint). These prove you're real and not just well-edited.

3. Build an online profile scouts can find

A scout in Kozhikode or a club in another state can't travel to every ground. A searchable online football profile — with your position, age, stats and video — lets them find you instantly. This is exactly what platforms like Talenta are built for: you create a free profile, and clubs, academies and scouts search and contact you directly.

4. Play where you'll be seen

Enter local "Sevens" tournaments, school and district competitions, and academy trials. Record this footage — real match clips against real opponents are far more convincing than training-ground tricks.

5. Be consistent and reachable

Keep your profile updated, respond quickly to messages, and stay disciplined. Opportunities often go to the player who is easiest to reach and most reliable — not always the most talented.
